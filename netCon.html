Why Networking Matters

The Main Reasons

01

Discover Hidden Opportunities

Many internships and jobs are never publicly posted—conversations surface projects, referrals, and emerging roles earlier than job boards.

02

Accelerate Learning & Clarity

Talking to practitioners helps you validate career interests, understand real skill expectations, and avoid investing time in the wrong paths.

03

Build Credibility & Support

Consistent, authentic interactions create a reputation; people remember your strengths and are more likely to advocate, mentor, or collaborate with you later.

Getting Started: Define Your Networking Goals

Clarify Your Focus

  • Identify industries, roles, or companies you want to explore.
  • Start strong by reviewing What you should know before you get start? for quick, essential tips.
  • Determine what you want to learn or achieve from each interaction.

Map Your Existing Network

  • List professors, alumni, classmates, and family connections in your target field.
  • Use LinkedIn to find UMSI alumni working in roles or companies you're curious about.

Set Weekly Micro-Goals

  • Commit to reaching out to 2-3 people per week.
  • Track your outreach and follow-ups in a simple spreadsheet or note app.
  • Celebrate small wins—every conversation is progress.

Student Q&A

How do I reach out to someone I don't know?

Start with a personalized message mentioning a shared connection or interest. Be specific about what you'd like to learn and keep it brief.

What if someone doesn't respond?

Don't take it personally! Follow up once after a week, then move on. Focus your energy on people who are responsive.

How do I maintain connections?

Send occasional updates about your progress, share relevant articles, or simply check in. Authentic relationships are built over time.
